Ukraine's fulfillment market hits 2.5B UAH ($60M) in 2026.
Forecast to 2030 — 7-8B UAH ($170-190M).

Yet most Western brands still treat Ukraine as "too risky to consider" — a 3-year-old assumption that no longer matches reality.

What actually happened on the ground:

→ 30+ active fulfillment operators, top 3 control ~45% of the market
→ Polish and Turkish 3PLs are entering — physical war risk has been priced in
→ 70% of operators run modern WMS (the rest are dying out fast)
→ Same-day fulfillment from Kyiv to most of Poland: 36-48 hours
→ COD-handling infrastructure is mature in ways the EU still hasn't matched
→ Pricing: storage from €16/m³, pick-pack from €0.45-0.65 — vs €0.90-1.20 in PL/DE

For Western e-commerce brands, this means three things:

1. Ukraine is no longer a peripheral logistics market — it's a competitive entry point to a 30M-consumer country.
2. Native-language returns handling, COD reconciliation, marketplace API integration — done locally is dramatically cheaper than via Polish proxies.
3. The "Ukraine = war = pause" narrative is outdated for fulfillment specifically. Operators run with backup power, distributed warehouses, and 99.5%+ uptime.
